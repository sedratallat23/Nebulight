Jack's First Mission

Flip through the cosmic photos!

Today was one of my dream days. I, Jack, was finally an astronaut, and this was my very first journey into space with my crew. “Hey guys, are you ready for today?” I asked.

Astronaut Mark sighed, “No… I’m sad. How will I live without pizza?” Astronaut Elvin laughed, “You don’t want to die because of a piece of pizza in space!”

Captain Kelvin spoke firmly, “Come on, get ready. Put on your spacesuits—we’ll board the spacecraft soon.” Inside the spacecraft, Commander Chris started the countdown: 5... 4... 3... 2... 1... 0! The rocket launched.

I was in charge of monitoring the instruments. Captain Kelvin and Elvin were controlling the spacecraft, while Mark was responsible for communication with Earth. I stared in wonder at the Earth—it looked so small from up here. Suddenly, I noticed an unusual bright flash from the Sun.

My thoughts were cut short by Mark’s urgent voice. “There’s a message from Earth,” he said. “Warning: a solar storm is approaching!” I asked nervously, “What will happen?” Mark explained, “During a solar storm, the Sun releases dangerous rays of light that travel at incredible speed—they can reach Earth in just 8 minutes.” Elvin added, “Not only that, the Sun also throws out fiery plasma clouds during storms.”

Captain Kelvin commanded, “We must go to the shelter room. It’s specially designed to protect us from the storm.” I shouted, “Then let’s go, everyone!” We stayed in the shelter until the storm ended. When it was finally safe, we left the room, completed our mission, and prepared to return.

We stayed in the shelter until the storm ended. When we came out, a few systems were still blinking with warnings, but Captain Kelvin and Elvin quickly fixed them. Soon, everything was back to normal.

The spacecraft landed safely back on Earth. As soon as we got out, Mark ran and shouted, “Pizza! Pizza!” He was truly addicted. The rest of us laughed as we began telling the story of our mission—especially how we survived the solar storm.
